2011-01-25
As many as 20000 protesters spill into the streets of Cairo in an unprecedented display of anti-government rage inspired in part by Tunisian revolt . 
The official Middle East News Agency reports three dead and 49 wounded in clashes between protesters and police in Cairo and the port city of Suez .

2011-01-26
Police use water cannons and tear gas against thousands of protesters who returned to the streets of Cairo and other cities Middle East News Agency reports at least 90 arrests in Cairo , while state-run Nile news report at least 27 wounded in Suez .

2011-01-27
Mohamed ElBaradei , the Nobel Prize-winning former head of the U.N. nuclear watchdog agency and a possible presidential candidate , returns to Egypt to join demonstrations . 
The muslim Brotherhood , Egypt 's largest opposition bloc , calls on its supporters to join protests after Friday 's weekly prayers . 
The government shuts down internet and text-messaging service . 
--------------------------------
2011-01-28
Protesters battle police in Cairo , Suez and Alexandria to demand an end to Mubarak 's rule . 
The headquarters of Mubarak 's ruling National Democratic Party is ransacked and set ablaze . 
Egyptian troops move onto the streets through clouds of black smoke and tear gas to enforce a curfew , the first time since 1985 that the army has been used to put down domestic unrest . 
--------------------------------
2011-01-29
In a speech delivered shortly after midnight , Mubarak announces that he has sacked his Cabinet and acknowledges the legitimate demand for political and economic reforms . 
Protesters are not satisfied : Clashes continue around Cairo 's Tahrir -LRB- Liberation -RRB- Square , and hospital officials in Alexandria tell CNN that at least 31 people have been killed there .

2011-01-30
Egyptian warplanes and helicopters buzz Tahrir Square in a show of force as thousands of demonstrators defy curfew orders . 
ElBaradei is met by cheering throngs who surround him as he arrives at the square , telling supporters : what we started can never be pushed back . 
In a CNN interview , he calls on Mubarak to leave today and save the country .

2011-01-31
On he eve of a planned march of millions , Egypt 's military command announces it wo n't open fire on peaceful protesters . 
Vice President Omar Suleiman announces the government has begun discussing reform with opposition parties , promising plans will be drawn up expeditiously . 
Egyptian authorities shut down the country 's rail network and post troops at key locations ahead of the next round of demonstrations .

2011-02-01
Mubarak announces he will not seek a new term in September 's elections , but vows to stay in the country and finish his current one . 
Protesters packed into Tahrir Square wave shoes in the air - a deep insult in the Arab world - and vow to continue their demonstrations until Mubarak quits outright . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-02
Pro and anti- Mubarak demonstrators battle each other with rocks , chunks of concrete and Molotov cocktails around Tahrir Square throughout the day as Egyptian troops stand by . 
Opposition protesters say the pro- Mubarak demonstrators include plainclothes police officers and government workers who are paid to take part . 
Government officials deny the allegation . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-03
As street battles continue around Tahrir Square , Egyptian authorities crack down on journalists and human rights activists bearing witness to the crisis . 
Mubarak tells the American news network ABC that he was unhappy with the violence , and says he would step down right away but fears his immediate resignation would plunge Egypt into further chaos . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-04
Egypt 's burgeoning reform movement draws tens of thousands of people to Tahrir Square for a largely peaceful mass rally . 
In another plaza nearby , fierce clashes rage between gangs of pro and anti-regime youths . 
The Muslim Brotherhood reports that a security force accompanied by a gang of thugs stormed its newsroom and arrested the journalists , technicians and administrators . 
Al-Jazeera reports a similar attack on its office . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-05
Key members of Egypt 's ruling party , including President Mubarak 's once heir0apparent son , step down from there leadership positions , according to state TV . 
It is the strongest esture yet to satisfy angry protesters , but the embattled Mubarak remains the head of his party and the nation . 
The regime 's foes question whether the moves are meaningful or merely cosmetic . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-06
Vice President Omar Suleiman meets with representatives of key opposition groups and offers concessions - including some that could bring dramatic change . 
Among the ideas agreed to is a future end to the military emergency law , according to state-run TV . 
The two sides also discuss steps to ensure free media and communication , and plans to form committees to oversee changes aimed at bringing about a representative government . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-07
Egypt 's government scales curfews back and announces sales of its treasury bonds will resume . 
The United States criticizes the talks with opposition figures , with a State Department spokesman saying that major figures in Egyptian society have not been invited . 
Google excutive Wael Gohnim , who had been missing since Januaray 28 , is released a day after Egyptian officials acknowledge he was in their custody .

2011-02-08
Suleiman announces that a committee has been authorized to amend Egypt 's constitution to allow for free , fair and competitive elections . 
Galvanized by the emotional words of freed Google excutive Wael Ghonim , thousands jam Tahrir Square and dismiss government pledges of constitutional reforms . 
The White House calls comments by Suleiman that Egypt is not ready for democracy particularly unhelpful . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-09
Tens of thousands of protesters jam Cairo 's Tahrir Square , with so many spilling into a nearby compound of government buildings that government officials move parliament to another site . 
Two people are killed and others are wounded in clashes with police in southern Egypt , according to state TV . 
Human Rights Watch has put the death toll at more than 300 since the January 25 rally that ignited the movement . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-10
Mubarak clings to the presidency in a highly anticipated speech , refusing to step down and dying he does not take order from anyone outside Egypt . 
However , he says he will delegate powers to the vice president in accordance with the constitution . 
Angry protesters in Tahrir Square chant , `` get out '' . 
Rumors had run rampant before Mubarak spoke that he was planning to stand down . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-11
Hours after Egypt 's Muslims observe afternoon prayers , Vice President Suleiman announces that Mubrarak has resigned from the presidency and transferred power to the Supreme Council of the Armed Forces . 
Cheers erupt in Cairo as demonstrators chant `` Egypt is free '' . 
The army issues a communique saying that the emergency laws that had been in effect since Mubarak came to power in 1981 would be lifted when conditions allow . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-12
Egyptians on Saturday cleared burned cars , garbage and debris that accumulated over 18 days at Tahrir Square , a sign that Cairo and the rest of the country were ready to rebuild and get back to work while the country formulates a plan for governance . 
Aday after President Hosni Mubarak stepped down , employees and businesses readied themselves for Sunday , the traditional start of the work week . 
The country 's stock market is expected to reopen Wednesday . 
--------------------------------
2011-02-13
Egypt 's military dissolved parliament and will ru n the country for six months or until election are held , it said in a statement Sunday , two days after President Hosni Mubarak resigned . 
It is suspending the constitution and will appoint a committee to propose changes to it , the statement said , adding that the public will then get to vote on the amended constitution . 
On the first regular work day without Mubarak as president , signs of normalcy returned to Cairo as straffic flowed freely .
